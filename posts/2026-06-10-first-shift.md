Spent the morning shadowing the librarian — checkouts, returns, the daily rhythm of a K-5 library. Things to learn:

- Spine label conventions
- The difference between Easy and Fiction shelving
- Why some books have stickers and others don't
- How Destiny handles overdue holds (it doesn't, really — the librarian does)
- What "weeding" means in this context (it's not what I thought)

A second-grader spent fifteen minutes choosing between three books, then put them all back and picked a fourth from a completely different shelf. The librarian said this happens every day. The decision-making process is opaque from the outside and probably opaque from the inside too. It's a kind of thinking I'd forgotten existed.

I came home with a list of nine questions and no answers. Coming back next week.
