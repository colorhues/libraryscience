## So, what did Diane and I do today?

Diane spent nearly 2 hours with me at Los Robles Library this afternoon. We spent most of that time thinking through and redesigning the layout of our wonderful library space. We shifted and reshaped the furniture, and I'm happy to report that we landed on a strong v1 layout.

The end result creates spaces for TK and K picture book titles, a transition space for early readers and 1st and 2nd grade titles, and finally a space for Fiction and Non Fiction titles for 3rd through 5th graders. We grouped the early readers with graphic novels and began arranging the graphic novels from beginner to more advanced levels.

Inspired by Jody Buckley's guidance, I chose to genrefy the entire collection. Our collection isn't big enough for a more rigorous Dewey Decimal system to make a huge impact. And, the genre based library spaces represent a compelling foundation on which to build that rigor as the library space evolves.

This work contributes to objective 4 listed below. Earlier this week, we also made progress against objective 3. We probably have about 200 new books to label with barcodes.

## The big operational goal

The big goal is to organize the library stacks so that books can be checked in, checked out, and general library usage tracked by student and classroom / grade level.

## What is needed to achieve this goal?

1. 🟢 **DONE:** The physical stacks are mirrored by the digital collection in Destiny.
2. 🟢 **DONE:** Destiny activities require functioning hardware, eg: a working scanner (or two).
3. 🟡 **IN PROGRESS:** print barcodes and catalog newly added books.
4. 🟡 **IN PROGRESS:** an organizational system that kids can easily learn and interpret.
